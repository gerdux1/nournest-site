What is a serviced apartment?

A whole home let by the night or month, with hotel-style cleaning, linen and support. How it differs from hotels, Airbnb and a regular short let.

Quick answer

A serviced apartment is a fully furnished apartment let by the night or by the month, combining the kitchen, living space and privacy of a home with the housekeeping, linen, wifi and concierge services of a hotel. Typical stays are 3 nights to 3 months. Per night, prices are similar to a hotel room of the same standard — but you get two to three times the space, a kitchen, a washer-dryer, and the option to settle in.

The simple definition

A serviced apartment is a fully furnished apartment let by the night or by the month. It includes the privacy and space of a home (bedroom, living room, kitchen, bathroom), plus the services and standards of a hotel (housekeeping, linen, wifi, guest support).

The difference from a typical Airbnb is professionalism: serviced apartments are run as an operation, not a side income — with consistent standards, formal contracts, insurance, dedicated cleaning and maintenance teams, and 24/7 issue response.

Who stays in a serviced apartment?

  • Business travellers on project stays — typically 1-3 weeks, want a kitchen and a desk.
  • Families on longer holidays — one apartment is cheaper than two hotel rooms and the kitchen saves on dining out.
  • People relocating — bridge accommodation between countries or while waiting for a long-term rental.
  • Hospital visits — guests adjacent to a London hospital for treatment or for visiting a relative.
  • Long-stay guests — 28+ nights at a monthly rate, often working remotely from London for a few months.
