Discover London

Where we'd send a friend.

50 London places we actually recommend — gathered over seven years of looking after guests in our apartments. Eat, drink, see, do. Practical answers to "what's nearby and worth your time".

Eat (6) Drink (4) See (5) Do (5)

Eat

From a Brick Lane bagel run at 2am to nose-to-tail British cooking in Clerkenwell.

Indian · Iconic

Dishoom Shoreditch

Shoreditch · 7 Boundary Street, E2 7JE · Old Street tube

The Bombay-Irani café you'll have heard of, and it deserves the hype. Bacon naan rolls before 11am, house black daal that's been on the menu since 2010. Walk-in queues move; the breakfast menu is the underrated win. There's also a King's Cross branch in a restored Victorian railway transit shed if you'd rather avoid the wait.

British · 24/7

Duck & Waffle

City · 40th floor, Heron Tower, EC2N 4AY · Liverpool Street

The 40th-floor view alone justifies the trip; the kitchen happens to be genuinely good. Open round the clock — duck egg en cocotte at 3am is its own kind of London experience. Pre-book a window table for sunset over the Square Mile and arrive a little early for a drink at the Sushi Samba bar one floor down.

British · Modern classic

St. John Restaurant

Smithfield · 26 St John Street, EC1M 4AY · Farringdon

Fergus Henderson's "nose-to-tail" temple — bone marrow on toast, Welsh rarebit, Eccles cakes. Stripped-white dining room, no music, the focus is the food. Lunch is the move if you can: less buzz, easier to get in, same exceptional cooking. Order whatever's on the daily-changing menu; trust the kitchen.

Bagel · Legendary

Beigel Bake

Brick Lane · 159 Brick Lane, E1 6SB · Shoreditch High Street

Open 24 hours, cash preferred, queue out the door at any time of day or night. The salt beef bagel with mustard and a pickle is the order. £6, eaten standing up on the pavement at 1am, is one of the more honestly memorable London moments. Stock up — they freeze well.

All-day · Australian

Granger & Co. Clerkenwell

Clerkenwell · 50 Sekforde Street, EC1R 0HA · Farringdon

Bill Granger's London outpost — ricotta hotcakes, scrambled eggs the way they ought to be, an honest cup of coffee. Sunny room, low-key vibe. Walk-ins on weekdays; book at weekends. For guests in Farringdon, Clerkenwell, or anywhere on the Northern Line, this is the easy breakfast answer.

Pub · Original gastropub

The Eagle Farringdon

Farringdon · 159 Farringdon Road, EC1R 3AL

The pub that invented the gastropub — opened in 1991, written into London's restaurant history. Mediterranean-leaning daily menu chalked on the board, order at the bar, find a table. Bife Ana steak sandwich if it's on. Loud, busy, deliberately unglamorous, exactly the point.

Filipino · Casual

Rapsa @ 100 Hoxton

Hoxton · 100 Hoxton Street, N1 6SH · Hoxton overground

Filipino comfort food done properly — adobo, sisig, lechon kawali — in a casual Hoxton dining room. One of London's better-kept secrets. Walk-ins usually fine on weekdays. Generous portions, low prices, a real cuisine you don't get in central London tourist spots.

Japanese · Modern

Sanjugo Shoreditch

Shoreditch · 35 Charlotte Road, EC2A 3PD · Shoreditch High Street

Modern Japanese small-plates and sushi without the City wallet hit. The robata grill is the move — Wagyu beef skewers, miso-glazed aubergine, charred shishito peppers. Smart cocktail list. Book ahead for weekends.

Café · Brunch

Roses Café

Shoreditch · 52 Old Street, EC1V 9AE

The neighbourhood brunch spot — eggs, sourdough, exceptional flat whites. Plant-heavy menu, dog-friendly, light-filled corner location. Walk-ins; weekends fill up by 11am. Five-minute walk from our Old Street apartments.

Wine bar · Small plates

Friends of Ours

Hoxton · 61 Pitfield Street, N1 6BU

Antipodean-influenced all-day spot — coffee in the morning, wine and small plates by evening. Counter seating, exposed brick, unfussy plates with serious cooking behind them. The chocolate fondant is famous for a reason.

Fish & chips · Classic

Poppies Fish & Chips

Spitalfields · 6-8 Hanbury Street, E1 6QR · Liverpool Street

1950s-style chippy done with proper craft — beer-battered cod, hand-cut chips, jellied eels if you're brave. The Spitalfields branch is the original. Eat in for the diner vibe, takeaway if you're queue-averse. Also branches in Camden and Soho.

Tapas · Casual

Morito

Exmouth Market · 32 Exmouth Market, EC1R 4QE · Farringdon

Sam and Sam Clark's little sister to Moro — tapas-style Spanish-Moroccan small plates. Walk-in only, expect to queue at 7pm Fridays. Order: the chorizo with potato & flat parsley salad, the crispy lamb shoulder, anything from the daily specials.

Café · Greek

Briki

Exmouth Market · 67 Exmouth Market, EC1R 4QL

Greek-style café — freddo cappuccinos, koulouri (Greek sesame bread rings), spanakopita to take away. Tiny, mostly standing. The under-publicised London Greek institution.

Indian · Kings Cross outpost

Dishoom King's Cross

King's Cross · 5 Stable Street, N1C 4AB · King's Cross St. Pancras

The largest Dishoom — a restored Victorian railway transit shed with three floors, a permit room, and the same Bombay-Irani menu as Shoreditch. Walk-in queues at peak times; bookings for groups of 6+. The breakfast bacon naan roll, again, is the order.

Italian · Stylish

Casa Tua King's Cross

King's Cross · 1 Lower Stable Street, N1C 4DR

Authentic Italian small plates, regional pastas, a serious wine list. Tucked into the King's Cross development behind Granary Square. Pre-theatre crowd at 6, dinner crowd from 8. Book ahead for window seats.

Drink

Four bars across four moods — speakeasy jazz, a quiet basement, a free skyline view, and a proper London pub.

Speakeasy · Cocktails

Nightjar

Shoreditch · 129 City Road, EC1V 1JB · Old Street

Through an unmarked door, downstairs into low light and live jazz. The cocktail menu reads like a short novel — every drink has provenance and craft. Always book ahead; walk-ins are tough on weekends. Sister venue Oriole in Smithfield runs the same playbook with a different soundtrack.

Basement · No menu

Happiness Forgets

Hoxton · 8-9 Hoxton Square, N1 6NU · Old Street

Tucked under a square in Hoxton. No menus — you tell them what you like (gin, citrus, smoky, sweet), they make it. Small, dark, no music too loud to talk over. Bookings only, two-hour slots. The bar that other London bartenders go to on their nights off.

Skyline · Free

Sky Garden

City · 20 Fenchurch Street, EC3M 8AF · Monument

Top of the "Walkie-Talkie" — three floors of indoor garden, terraces, bars. Free if you book a slot online a few days ahead, otherwise the bar takes walk-ins (paid drinks). Sunset bookings go fast. The view across the river to Tower Bridge and the Shard is the best free view in London.

Pub · Local

The Three Kings

Clerkenwell · 7 Clerkenwell Close, EC1R 0DY · Farringdon

A perfect mid-week London pub — small, old, no pretence. Decent ales, a good wine list, no music, regulars at the bar. Around the corner from St. John and the Eagle, so it pairs with a Clerkenwell food crawl. Sit outside in the alley in summer.

Late-night · Live music

The Blues Kitchen

Shoreditch · 134-146 Curtain Road, EC2A 3AR

Live blues, deep-fried-everything menu, a long bourbon list. Free entry, live music seven nights a week, open until 3am Friday/Saturday. The Camden and Brixton branches do the same in different postcodes; Shoreditch has the best room.

Late-night · Karaoke

Howl At The Moon

Hoxton · 178 Hoxton Street, N1 5LH

Dive bar energy, dollar-bills-stapled-to-the-ceiling aesthetic, surprisingly good cocktails. Karaoke night on Wednesdays is legendary. Open until 2am most nights, free entry, no dress code. The post-Nightjar destination.

Cocktails · Dance floor

The Piano Works

Farringdon · 113-117 Farringdon Road, EC1R 3BX

Two duelling grand pianos, live band taking requests until 2am, surprisingly serious food earlier in the evening. Free entry on most nights, table bookings worth it for groups. The London night out you didn't know you wanted.

All-day bar · Hotel-style

Bourne & Hollingsworth Buildings

Clerkenwell · 42 Northampton Road, EC1R 0HU

The garden-room bar — botanicals overhead, all-day cocktails, brunch on Saturdays. Reservations recommended for evening. Sister venue B&H Club in Fitzrovia has the original speakeasy vibe, but this one's the easy one to get into.

Spanish · Tapas + sherry

Camino King's Cross

King's Cross · 3 Varnishers Yard, N1 9FD

Genuine Spanish tapas + an outdoor terrace + a long sherry list. The King's Cross branch is the original; expanded to Monument, Bankside, Blackfriars. Late-night tapas Friday/Saturday. A solid post-Eurostar arrival meal.

See

Five places we send guests when the weather turns or they want to actually look at something.

Museum · Free · Iconic

The British Museum

Bloomsbury · Great Russell Street, WC1B 3DG · Tottenham Court Road

Free, vast, world-class. The Rosetta Stone, the Parthenon sculptures, the Egyptian collection. Don't try to do it all — pick two or three galleries, an hour each. The Great Court (Norman Foster's glass roof) is worth seeing in itself. Quieter early on weekdays; queues build by 11am.

Museum · Free · Design

Victoria & Albert Museum

South Kensington · Cromwell Road, SW7 2RL

The world's leading museum of art and design. The fashion gallery, the cast courts, the William Morris room — all free. Paid temporary exhibitions are usually worth it (book online). Café in the original 19th-century refreshment rooms is the under-mentioned highlight. Pairs with the V&A's neighbours: Natural History Museum and Science Museum, all on the same block.

Gallery · Modern

Royal Academy of Arts

Mayfair · Burlington House, Piccadilly, W1J 0BD

Britain's oldest art school, founded 1768, still running blockbuster shows out of Burlington House. The Summer Exhibition (June-August) hangs thousands of works by everyone from RAs to first-time exhibitors — buy what you love. Walk-in Friends membership gets you into all exhibitions for the year if you'll visit more than three times.

Architecture · Cultural

Barbican Centre

Barbican · Silk Street, EC2Y 8DS

Europe's largest multi-arts venue — theatre, dance, classical music, film, art exhibitions, all under one Grade II-listed brutalist roof. Worth a visit just for the building (lakeside terrace, conservatory open Sundays). Check what's on; tickets often available same-day. Free public art across the estate.

Cathedral · Iconic

St. Paul's Cathedral

City · St. Paul's Churchyard, EC4M 8AD

Christopher Wren's masterpiece, finished 1710. Climb the dome — 528 steps to the Golden Gallery, the best view of the City you can get without paying for the Shard. Free for worship (early morning Evensong on weekdays); ticketed entry for sightseeing. Quieter on weekday afternoons.

Royal · Iconic

Buckingham Palace

Westminster · SW1A 1AA · Green Park / Victoria

State Rooms open July–September only — book ahead, they sell out. Changing of the Guard 11am on alternate days (check schedule). The walk through St. James's Park to the Palace is the better London moment than the ceremony itself.

Library · Free · World-class

The British Library

King's Cross · 96 Euston Road, NW1 2DB · King's Cross St. Pancras

Free to enter. The Treasures gallery has the Magna Carta, Shakespeare's First Folio, Beatles handwritten lyrics. Excellent café and a serious bookshop. Workspace-friendly tables if you need a quiet hour. Pairs with Coal Drops Yard ten minutes' walk away.

Gallery · Contemporary

Victoria Miro Gallery

Old Street · 16 Wharf Road, N1 7RW

One of London's serious contemporary art galleries — Yayoi Kusama, Grayson Perry, Chris Ofili shown regularly. Free to enter. The canalside garden is open in summer and worth the trip alone. Smaller crowds than the Tates; better for actually looking.

Palace · Royal residence

Kensington Palace

Kensington Gardens · W8 4PX · High Street Kensington

Where William and Kate live, where Princess Diana once lived, where Queen Victoria was born. The State Apartments and the Diana Fashion Exhibition are open to visitors. Wonderful for the gardens alone — Kensington Gardens runs into Hyde Park, all free.

Gallery · Free · Hidden gem

Serpentine Galleries

Kensington Gardens · W2 3XA · Lancaster Gate

Two contemporary art galleries inside Kensington Gardens — Serpentine North and the original Serpentine across the bridge. Free, ambitious programming, world-class architects' annual Pavilion installations (summer). Pair with a Hyde Park walk and lunch at the Lido café.

Theatre · Dance

Sadler's Wells Theatre

Angel · Rosebery Avenue, EC1R 4TN

London's leading dance venue — contemporary, ballet, hip-hop, international tours. The Peacock Theatre and Lilian Baylis Studio host smaller productions. Programmed bravely; if you don't recognise the choreographer, that's often the better ticket.

Museum · Niche

The Postal Museum & Mail Rail

Clerkenwell · 15-20 Phoenix Place, WC1X 0DA

Niche but brilliant — five centuries of British postal history plus a 15-minute ride on the underground Mail Rail train (London's secret tube). Strong appeal for families with kids 5-10. Book Mail Rail timed slots ahead.

Do

Five outdoor experiences and weekend rituals that show you the London that locals know.

Walk · Free · Iconic

Regent's Canal

Little Venice → Camden Lock · ~45 mins flat walk

The towpath from Little Venice to Camden — narrowboats, willow trees, the back of London Zoo, a working lock or three. Brunch first at one of the Little Venice cafés; finish at Camden Market for street food and a pint by the lock. Bikes welcome (slow on weekends). One of the best London walks no tourist knows about.

Market · Sunday only

Columbia Road Flower Market

East London · Columbia Road, E2 7RG · Sundays 8am-3pm

One of the few London markets where the regulars and the visitors mix happily. Buy peonies, eat a bagel from Jones Dairy, drink coffee on a stoop. The pubs open early. Best by 9am — by midday it's shoulder-to-shoulder. Pairs with a Brick Lane afternoon; both are walking distance from our Shoreditch apartments.

Market · Weekend food

Maltby Street Market

Bermondsey · Maltby Street, SE1 3PA · Saturdays 10-5, Sundays 11-4

The grown-up's Borough Market — tucked into railway arches in Bermondsey, less crowded, more food traders worth eating from. Wine bars, oysters, sourdough, the original St. John bakery. Walk it off on Bermondsey Street afterward — White Cube gallery and a row of independent shops.

Green space · Wild

Hampstead Heath

North London · NW3 1TH · Hampstead tube

320 hectares of properly wild London — not a manicured Royal Park. Walk to Parliament Hill for the skyline view (it's used in postcards because it's that good). Three swimming ponds (one men's, one women's, one mixed) if you're brave; year-round, cold. Kenwood House at the north end is a free Robert Adam mansion with Rembrandts.

Park · Manicured

The Regent's Park

Marylebone / NW1 · Regent's Park Road · Baker Street or Regent's Park tube

The most beautiful of the Royal Parks — formal rose gardens, the boating lake, the open-air theatre, and London Zoo at the northern end. Primrose Hill on the north edge has the best central skyline view (and a row of perfect Sunday pubs at its base). Bring a book and a flask.

Market · Daily

Camden Market

Camden Town · Camden Lock Place, NW1 8AF · Camden Town tube

Lock-side food market, antiques, vintage stalls, street food from every corner of the world. Best on weekday afternoons (locals' rhythm); avoid Saturday lunch unless you like crowds. Walk in via Regent's Canal from Little Venice for the proper approach.

Zoo · Iconic · Family

London Zoo

Regent's Park · NW1 4RY · Camden Town / Regent's Park

The world's oldest scientific zoo, 200+ years old. Penguin Beach, the gorilla kingdom, the reptile house (Harry Potter fans will recognise it). Book online for ~15% discount on day-of prices. A full half-day with kids; two hours with adults who like animals.

Neighbourhood · Walking

Notting Hill

West London · Portobello Road, W11 1LJ · Notting Hill Gate

Portobello Road Market on Saturdays is the main event — antiques, vintage, street food, the pastel houses you've seen in the films. Stop at Books for Cooks for an unbeatable £8 set lunch. Sundays are quieter, perfect for the houses and the cafés without the market crush.

Cinema · Independent

Everyman King's Cross

King's Cross · Lewis Cubitt Square, N1C 4DH

Sofa-seat cinema with table service — the Everyman chain at its best. Curated programming alongside mainstream releases. Pair with dinner at Dishoom or Casa Tua five minutes away. A genuinely civilised London cinema night.

House museum · Free

Leighton House

Holland Park · 12 Holland Park Road, W14 8LZ · Kensington High Street

The Victorian artist Frederic Leighton's home — the Arab Hall with its mosaic walls is one of the most unexpected rooms in London. Recently restored, ticketed entry. Pair with a walk through Holland Park (open until dusk; peacocks roam free).
