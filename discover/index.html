Discover London

Where we'd send a friend.

A handful of London places we actually recommend — gathered over seven years of looking after guests in our apartments. Eat, drink, see, do. Practical answers to "what's nearby and worth your time".

Eat (6) Drink (4) See (5) Do (5)

Eat

From a Brick Lane bagel run at 2am to nose-to-tail British cooking in Clerkenwell.

Indian · Iconic

Dishoom Shoreditch

Shoreditch · 7 Boundary Street, E2 7JE · Old Street tube

The Bombay-Irani café you'll have heard of, and it deserves the hype. Bacon naan rolls before 11am, house black daal that's been on the menu since 2010. Walk-in queues move; the breakfast menu is the underrated win. There's also a King's Cross branch in a restored Victorian railway transit shed if you'd rather avoid the wait.

British · 24/7

Duck & Waffle

City · 40th floor, Heron Tower, EC2N 4AY · Liverpool Street

The 40th-floor view alone justifies the trip; the kitchen happens to be genuinely good. Open round the clock — duck egg en cocotte at 3am is its own kind of London experience. Pre-book a window table for sunset over the Square Mile and arrive a little early for a drink at the Sushi Samba bar one floor down.

British · Modern classic

St. John Restaurant

Smithfield · 26 St John Street, EC1M 4AY · Farringdon

Fergus Henderson's "nose-to-tail" temple — bone marrow on toast, Welsh rarebit, Eccles cakes. Stripped-white dining room, no music, the focus is the food. Lunch is the move if you can: less buzz, easier to get in, same exceptional cooking. Order whatever's on the daily-changing menu; trust the kitchen.

Bagel · Legendary

Beigel Bake

Brick Lane · 159 Brick Lane, E1 6SB · Shoreditch High Street

Open 24 hours, cash preferred, queue out the door at any time of day or night. The salt beef bagel with mustard and a pickle is the order. £6, eaten standing up on the pavement at 1am, is one of the more honestly memorable London moments. Stock up — they freeze well.

All-day · Australian

Granger & Co. Clerkenwell

Clerkenwell · 50 Sekforde Street, EC1R 0HA · Farringdon

Bill Granger's London outpost — ricotta hotcakes, scrambled eggs the way they ought to be, an honest cup of coffee. Sunny room, low-key vibe. Walk-ins on weekdays; book at weekends. For guests in Farringdon, Clerkenwell, or anywhere on the Northern Line, this is the easy breakfast answer.

Pub · Original gastropub

The Eagle Farringdon

Farringdon · 159 Farringdon Road, EC1R 3AL

The pub that invented the gastropub — opened in 1991, written into London's restaurant history. Mediterranean-leaning daily menu chalked on the board, order at the bar, find a table. Bife Ana steak sandwich if it's on. Loud, busy, deliberately unglamorous, exactly the point.

Drink

Four bars across four moods — speakeasy jazz, a quiet basement, a free skyline view, and a proper London pub.

Speakeasy · Cocktails

Nightjar

Shoreditch · 129 City Road, EC1V 1JB · Old Street

Through an unmarked door, downstairs into low light and live jazz. The cocktail menu reads like a short novel — every drink has provenance and craft. Always book ahead; walk-ins are tough on weekends. Sister venue Oriole in Smithfield runs the same playbook with a different soundtrack.

Basement · No menu

Happiness Forgets

Hoxton · 8-9 Hoxton Square, N1 6NU · Old Street

Tucked under a square in Hoxton. No menus — you tell them what you like (gin, citrus, smoky, sweet), they make it. Small, dark, no music too loud to talk over. Bookings only, two-hour slots. The bar that other London bartenders go to on their nights off.

Skyline · Free

Sky Garden

City · 20 Fenchurch Street, EC3M 8AF · Monument

Top of the "Walkie-Talkie" — three floors of indoor garden, terraces, bars. Free if you book a slot online a few days ahead, otherwise the bar takes walk-ins (paid drinks). Sunset bookings go fast. The view across the river to Tower Bridge and the Shard is the best free view in London.

Pub · Local

The Three Kings

Clerkenwell · 7 Clerkenwell Close, EC1R 0DY · Farringdon

A perfect mid-week London pub — small, old, no pretence. Decent ales, a good wine list, no music, regulars at the bar. Around the corner from St. John and the Eagle, so it pairs with a Clerkenwell food crawl. Sit outside in the alley in summer.

See

Five places we send guests when the weather turns or they want to actually look at something.

Museum · Free · Iconic

The British Museum

Bloomsbury · Great Russell Street, WC1B 3DG · Tottenham Court Road

Free, vast, world-class. The Rosetta Stone, the Parthenon sculptures, the Egyptian collection. Don't try to do it all — pick two or three galleries, an hour each. The Great Court (Norman Foster's glass roof) is worth seeing in itself. Quieter early on weekdays; queues build by 11am.

Museum · Free · Design

Victoria & Albert Museum

South Kensington · Cromwell Road, SW7 2RL

The world's leading museum of art and design. The fashion gallery, the cast courts, the William Morris room — all free. Paid temporary exhibitions are usually worth it (book online). Café in the original 19th-century refreshment rooms is the under-mentioned highlight. Pairs with the V&A's neighbours: Natural History Museum and Science Museum, all on the same block.

Gallery · Modern

Royal Academy of Arts

Mayfair · Burlington House, Piccadilly, W1J 0BD

Britain's oldest art school, founded 1768, still running blockbuster shows out of Burlington House. The Summer Exhibition (June-August) hangs thousands of works by everyone from RAs to first-time exhibitors — buy what you love. Walk-in Friends membership gets you into all exhibitions for the year if you'll visit more than three times.

Architecture · Cultural

Barbican Centre

Barbican · Silk Street, EC2Y 8DS

Europe's largest multi-arts venue — theatre, dance, classical music, film, art exhibitions, all under one Grade II-listed brutalist roof. Worth a visit just for the building (lakeside terrace, conservatory open Sundays). Check what's on; tickets often available same-day. Free public art across the estate.

Cathedral · Iconic

St. Paul's Cathedral

City · St. Paul's Churchyard, EC4M 8AD

Christopher Wren's masterpiece, finished 1710. Climb the dome — 528 steps to the Golden Gallery, the best view of the City you can get without paying for the Shard. Free for worship (early morning Evensong on weekdays); ticketed entry for sightseeing. Quieter on weekday afternoons.

Do

Five outdoor experiences and weekend rituals that show you the London that locals know.

Walk · Free · Iconic

Regent's Canal

Little Venice → Camden Lock · ~45 mins flat walk

The towpath from Little Venice to Camden — narrowboats, willow trees, the back of London Zoo, a working lock or three. Brunch first at one of the Little Venice cafés; finish at Camden Market for street food and a pint by the lock. Bikes welcome (slow on weekends). One of the best London walks no tourist knows about.

Market · Sunday only

Columbia Road Flower Market

East London · Columbia Road, E2 7RG · Sundays 8am-3pm

One of the few London markets where the regulars and the visitors mix happily. Buy peonies, eat a bagel from Jones Dairy, drink coffee on a stoop. The pubs open early. Best by 9am — by midday it's shoulder-to-shoulder. Pairs with a Brick Lane afternoon; both are walking distance from our Shoreditch apartments.

Market · Weekend food

Maltby Street Market

Bermondsey · Maltby Street, SE1 3PA · Saturdays 10-5, Sundays 11-4

The grown-up's Borough Market — tucked into railway arches in Bermondsey, less crowded, more food traders worth eating from. Wine bars, oysters, sourdough, the original St. John bakery. Walk it off on Bermondsey Street afterward — White Cube gallery and a row of independent shops.

Green space · Wild

Hampstead Heath

North London · NW3 1TH · Hampstead tube

320 hectares of properly wild London — not a manicured Royal Park. Walk to Parliament Hill for the skyline view (it's used in postcards because it's that good). Three swimming ponds (one men's, one women's, one mixed) if you're brave; year-round, cold. Kenwood House at the north end is a free Robert Adam mansion with Rembrandts.

Park · Manicured

The Regent's Park

Marylebone / NW1 · Regent's Park Road · Baker Street or Regent's Park tube

The most beautiful of the Royal Parks — formal rose gardens, the boating lake, the open-air theatre, and London Zoo at the northern end. Primrose Hill on the north edge has the best central skyline view (and a row of perfect Sunday pubs at its base). Bring a book and a flask.
