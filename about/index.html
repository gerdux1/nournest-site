About

A small London business, director-led and quietly run.

NourNest manages a hand-picked collection of London apartments and looks after the guests who stay in them. We are not a franchise. We are not a platform. We are a team that holds the keys.

NourNest Ltd was founded by Gerda Micke in 2019. The name was chosen because the work — at its best — is about making a London apartment feel like a nest the moment a guest walks in.

What we do

We sign management agreements with London landlords, list their apartments on direct channels and major travel platforms, and run the day-to-day operation: pricing, guest communication, cleaning, maintenance, compliance, and the small touches that turn a flat into a stay people remember.

We currently look after 49 apartments across central and greater London. Most of our owners came to us through word of mouth.

How we run things

  • One named contact. Gerda Micke is the director and the person accountable for every apartment. Not a call centre, not a rotating account manager.
  • Independently operated. Each apartment is managed by our own team, not subcontracted. We know which heating quirk lives in which flat.
  • 24/7 guest support. WhatsApp first, phone if it's urgent. Someone who can actually fix it, not just route the message.
  • Compliance-first. Gas, electrical, fire safety, insurance, licensing — handled before it becomes a problem.
  • Quarterly owner reporting. What we did, what's upcoming, what was spent, what we'd recommend.

What we don't do

  • Franchise. Every NourNest apartment is run by us, not a sub-operator.
  • Surprise fees. The management fee is agreed up front, in writing.
  • Vendor kickbacks. We don't take commission on works done for your apartment.
  • Public reference your home. We don't post addresses, owner names, or anything that identifies you.

The team

The business is small by design. We work with a tight team of housekeepers, maintenance specialists, and trusted London trades who have been with us repeatedly. We don't grow headcount until we can grow it without dropping standards.
